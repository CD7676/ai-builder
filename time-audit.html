Automation opportunities

Each one is an outcome — what the world looks like after AI handles it. Ranked by impact (hours reclaimed × tie to top goal). All draft estimates.

Auto-01 / Medium lift / ~3.0 hrs reclaimed / wk Draft

My job pipeline tells me where every role stands without me opening Gmail.

Today
Pipeline status lives in scattered Gmail threads. Don asks “where's Hyland?” and I scroll. Recruiters fall through gaps because there's no chase calendar.
Automated
An n8n workflow watches Gmail for recruiter senders and parses each thread into an Airtable / Notion pipeline (company, role, recruiter, stage, last touch, next action, my interest 1–10). Claude does the parsing. A Monday-morning email gives the “this week” view: who to chase, who's stale, what's new. This is the “systematic job search engine” from CLAUDE.md, made real.
Why first
Hits Goal 2 directly, uses tools I already know (n8n, Gmail, Claude), and pays back every single week of the search.
Auto-02 / Quick win / ~2.0 hrs reclaimed / wk Draft

Recruiter inbound gets pre-triaged into yes / maybe / pass before I touch it.

Today
Each recruiter email is a 5–10 minute decision: read JD, judge fit, draft response, schedule. I do this 2–4 times per week, often during work hours.
Automated
A Claude-powered Gmail filter scores incoming recruiter emails against my stated profile (NYC-based, B2B software focus, late-stage / scaleup / PortCo exec) and auto-drafts a polite pass for clear no-fits, a “tell me more” for maybes, and a “let's schedule” for strong fits. I review and send in one click.
Why this
Builds on my existing AI-draft setup. Removes the ambient cognitive cost of recruiter triage.
Auto-03 / Quick win / ~1.0 hr reclaimed / wk Draft

Every coaching session with Don leaves a structured artifact, not just an email.

Today
Don sends a recap email after each session (re-routed to personal Gmail on Apr 2). I read it, reply “thanks,” then it disappears into the inbox. Insights don't compound across sessions.
Automated
A Claude skill ingests Don's recap emails, tags themes (positioning, recruiter strategy, interview reps), and appends them to a single rolling “Coaching Notes” doc with action items. The same skill highlights what's still open from prior sessions.
Why this
I'm already paying for the coaching. The bottleneck is retention & follow-through, not insight quality.
Auto-04 / Big build / ~5.0 hrs reclaimed / wk Draft

My PE deal work gets the same AI lift as my personal life.

Today
Every AI build in the email data is personal (digest, HN, settlement reminder, daily quote). My three Claude Code agents (diligence-researcher, memo-drafter, model-reviewer) sit unused — no diligence runs, no memo drafts in the data.
Automated
Pick one PG deal stage (e.g. CIM intake) and build the AI workflow that handles it end-to-end on my work machine: PDF in, one-page summary out, with key risks flagged, comp set sketched, and questions for management surfaced. Same pattern for IC memo first drafts, model sanity-checks, recurring weekly market-update digests for the team.
Why this
Goal 1 says “professionally and personally.” Right now it's only personally. This is the half that compounds — into either being indispensable at PG, or being a credible AI builder when interviewing for ops roles.
Auto-05 / Quick win / ~1.5 hrs reclaimed / wk Draft

The job-alert firehose collapses into one ranked weekly email, not 50 daily ones.

Today
LinkedIn (3–5 / day), Jobright (1–2 / day), Glassdoor, Kimble, Greville — 50+ alert emails per week. I scan most and act on almost none.
Automated
Extends the Daily Email Digest: alerts get parsed for role + company + comp + location, scored against my target profile, deduped across sources, and rolled into a Friday email with the top 5–10 worth a closer look. Everything else auto-archives.
Why this
I already built the digest. This is one more parsing layer on top.
Auto-06 / Medium lift / ~1.0 hr reclaimed / wk Draft

Networking calls produce a structured record, not just “Thanks for talking” emails.

Today
Calls with Andrew Schmitt (Builders VC), Kyle Bryant (Fractional AI), Jeremy Noskin (Alternative Payments / Ctrl-Tab), etc. Each one ends with a short thank-you and then nothing structured. Hard to remember what each person actually does or what next-step I committed to.

Wispr Flow + a Claude skill: dictate a 60-second post-call brain dump, Claude converts it into a contact card (who, role, themes, next action, when to follow up). Feeds the same Notion / Airtable as the job pipeline. Six months from now I have a real network I can actually query.
Why this
