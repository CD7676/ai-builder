My job pipeline tells me where every role stands without me opening Gmaildraft
Medium lift ~3 hrs/wk
Today
Pipeline status lives in scattered Gmail threads. Don asks "where's Hyland?" and you scroll. Recruiters fall through gaps because there's no chase calendar.
Automated
An n8n workflow watches Gmail for recruiter senders and parses them into an Airtable/Notion pipeline (company, role, recruiter, stage, last touch, next action, my interest 1–10). Claude does the parsing. A Monday-morning email gives you the "this week" view: who to chase, who's stale, what's new. This is the "systematic job search engine" from your CLAUDE.md, made real.
Why this first
Hits Goal 2 directly, uses tools you already know (n8n, Gmail, Claude), and pays back every single week of the search.
Recruiter inbound gets pre-triaged into yes / maybe / pass before I touch itdraft
Quick win ~2 hrs/wk
Today
Each recruiter email is a 5–10 minute decision: read JD, judge fit, draft response, schedule. You do this 2–4 times per week, often during work hours.
Automated
A Claude-powered Gmail filter scores incoming recruiter emails against your stated preferences (NYC-based, B2B software focus, late-stage / scaleup / PortCo exec) and auto-drafts a polite pass for clear no-fits, a "tell me more" for maybes, and a "let's schedule" for strong fits. You review and send in one click.
Why this
Builds on your existing AI-draft setup. Removes ambient cognitive cost of recruiter triage.
