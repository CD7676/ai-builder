What I'm building

Six automations drawn from the audit, ranked by hours reclaimed × fit with Goal #1. The top of the list is the one build that hits the professional half of consumer-to-builder; the bottom of the list still compounds.

Build-001 / Big build / ~5.0 hrs reclaimed / wk Not started

My PE deal work gets the same AI lift as my personal life.

Today
Every AI automation I've shipped is personal — digest, HN, quote, settlement reminder. My three Claude Code agents (diligence-researcher, memo-drafter, model-reviewer) sit unused. Zero deal-work activity in the data.
Automated
Pick one PG deal stage — CIM intake — and build the AI workflow end-to-end on the work box. PDF in, one-page summary out: risks flagged, comp set sketched, management questions surfaced. Same pattern for IC memo first drafts, model sanity-checks, and recurring weekly market-update digests for the team. This is the build that makes the portfolio real.
Build-002 / Medium lift / ~3.0 hrs reclaimed / wk Not started

My job pipeline tells me where every role stands, without me opening Gmail.

Today
Pipeline lives in scattered Gmail threads. Don asks “where did Hyland end up?” and I scroll. Recruiters fall through the gaps because no chase calendar exists.
Automated
An n8n workflow watches Gmail for recruiter senders and parses each thread into an Airtable pipeline: company, role, recruiter, stage, last touch, next action, interest score 1–10. Claude does the parsing. A Monday-morning email gives the week view — who to chase, who's stale, what's new. The “systematic job search engine” from CLAUDE.md, made real.
Build-003 / Quick win / ~2.0 hrs reclaimed / wk Not started

Recruiter inbound gets pre-triaged into yes / maybe / pass before I touch it.

Today
Each recruiter email is a 5–10 minute decision: read the JD, judge fit, draft a response, schedule. I do this 2–4 times per week, usually during work hours.
Automated
A Claude-powered Gmail filter scores incoming recruiter emails against my stated profile (NYC, B2B software, late-stage / scaleup / PortCo exec) and auto-drafts three responses: a polite pass for clear no-fits, a “tell me more” for maybes, and a “let's schedule” for strong fits. I review and send in one click.
Build-004 / Quick win / ~1.5 hrs reclaimed / wk Not started

The job-alert firehose collapses into one ranked weekly email, not fifty daily ones.

Today
LinkedIn (3–5/day), Jobright (1–2/day), Glassdoor, Kimble, Greville — 50+ alert emails per week. I scan most and act on almost none.
Automated
Extend the Daily Email Digest I already built. Alerts get parsed for role, company, comp, and location; scored against my target profile; deduped across sources; and rolled into a Friday email with the top 5–10 worth a closer look. Everything else auto-archives.
Build-005 / Medium lift / ~1.0 hr reclaimed / wk Not started

Networking calls produce a structured record, not just a “thanks for talking” email.

Today
Calls with Andrew Schmitt (Builders VC), Kyle Bryant (Fractional AI), Jeremy Noskin (Alternative Payments) — each ends in a short thank-you and then nothing structured. Six months later it's hard to remember what each person does or what next step I committed to.
Automated
Wispr Flow plus a Claude skill. Dictate a 60-second post-call brain dump; Claude turns it into a contact card — who, role, themes, next action, when to follow up — and feeds the same Airtable as the job pipeline. Six months from now I have a real network I can query.
Build-006 / Quick win / ~1.0 hr reclaimed / wk Not started

Every coaching session with Don leaves a structured artifact, not just an email.

Today
Don sends a recap after each session. I read it, reply “thanks,” and the insights disappear into the inbox. Nothing compounds session to session.
Automated
A Claude skill ingests Don's recap emails, tags themes (positioning, recruiter strategy, interview reps), and appends them to a rolling “Coaching Notes” doc with action items. The same skill flags what's still open from prior sessions.
Build-007 / Medium lift / ~2.0 hrs reclaimed / wk Not started

For every interesting job, I have a ranked list of who to reach out to and how warm the path is.

Today
The job-search pipeline ends at the sheet. For each interesting role, manually opening LinkedIn, going to the company's People tab, scrolling for 2nd-degree connections, mentally cross-referencing who in my network might know them, then guessing a corporate email is 15–30 min per role. Most “interested” roles get applied to via the website with no warm path attempted.
Automated
For each role I mark interested in the Jobs sheet, Claude opens the company's LinkedIn People tab via Chrome MCP, reads the 2nd-degree connections it surfaces, and cross-references against my connection spreadsheet (annotated with 1–5 confidence per person). Output: a ranked list per role of who to reach out to, who to ask for the intro, and a guessed corporate email via Hunter.io for cold paths. Lands on a new “Outreach Plan” tab in the same sheet. Closes the loop from “scored opportunity” to “first-touch action.”
